Sausage and Shrimp Gumbo

Description

Gumbo is one of those dishes that is warm, full of flavor and everyone is happy to see a large pot on the stove. Once you try this delicious recipe you won’t need an occasion to make it again

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 cup all purpose flour
  • 6 cups chicken stock
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, finely diced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme
  • 1 tsp gumbo filé powder
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1½ tsp salt

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (218°C).
  2. Spread the all-purpose flour evenly in a cast iron skillet.
  3. Transfer to the preheated oven and toast for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it reaches a deep cinnamon color.
  4. Remove from the oven and transfer to a bowl.
  5. Whisk in 1 cup of chicken stock to form a smooth mixture. Set aside.
  6. In a Dutch oven, heat 3 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  7. Add celery, bell pepper, onion, and jalapeño, then sauté until soft and translucent (about 5 minutes).
  8. Stir in garlic and sliced andouille sausage, then cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. Stir in the toasted flour mixture (roux) and cook for 2 more minutes, stirring constantly.
  10. Pour in the remaining 5 cups of chicken stock and bring the mixture to a boil.
  11. Add salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, gumbo filé, thyme, and bay leaves.
  12. Mix well, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30 minutes.
  13. After 30 minutes, add the shrimp, cover, and cook for an additional 10 minutes until shrimp is pink and fully cooked.
  14. Remove bay leaves, then ladle the gumbo into bowls.
  15. Serve hot over white rice, garnished with fresh parsley or green onions.
